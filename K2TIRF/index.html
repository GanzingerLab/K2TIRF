The K2 TIRF microscope

Commercially available fluorescence microscopes that are compatible with single-molecule experiments are often very expensive and/or lack flexibility. In recent years, a number of open-source projects emerged within the (single-molecule) fluorescence microscopy community, facilitating the planning and setting up of a custom microscope. These projects can be combined with each other or with commercial solutions, depending on the experimental requirements, the infrastructure around the lab (workshops, instrument developers), the available budget and workforce.



Our single-molecule TIRF microscope K2 is based on the miCube and includes several additions:
  • Custom emission image splitter to record images simultaneously at three different optical wavelengths
  • Flip-in lens in the excitation path for photobleaching experiments (FRAP)
  • qgFocus: our implementation of the pgFocus focus stabilization system
  • Beamshaping device (piShaper) for flat-top illumination over a big field of view (see Stehr, Stein et al.)
  • Sample stage: Three-axis piezo stick-slip positioner (Smaract SLS5252) wih nanometer resolution and 30mm travel range
  • Objective-heater and plexiglass housing for live-cell imaging
